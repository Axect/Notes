F-Divergence

F-Divergence1. Definition2. Example via two gaussian3. Plug-in Estimator

1. Definition

(1)Df(P‖Q)≡∫Ωf(p(x)q(x))q(x)dx

  • P,Q : probability distributions over a space Ω

  • f : convex function such that f(x) is finite ∀x>0, f(1)=0

    f(1)=0 means p(x)=q(x) ⇒ No divergence

  • For example, f(t)=−log⁡t (Reverse KL-divergence) or f(t)=tlog⁡t (KL-divergence)

  • F-divergence measures the difference of P, Q in support of Q (supp(Q))

    • It seems like KL-divergence, but f-divergence is more general

2. Example via two gaussian

  • For example, consider two gaussian distributions

    (2)p(x)∼N(x|−1,0.22)q(x)∼N(x|1,22)

    (3)Df(1)(P‖Q)≃∫−33−q(x)ln⁡(p(x)q(x))dx≃4.34×101Df(2)(P‖Q)≃∫−33p(x)ln⁡(p(x)q(x))dx≃2.31

  • Also, consider two gaussian distributions again

    (4)p(x)∼N(x|−1,0.22)q(x)∼N(x|1,0.22)

    (5)Df(1)(P‖Q)≃∫−33−q(x)ln⁡(p(x)q(x))dx≃5.00×101Df(2)(P‖Q)≃∫−33p(x)ln⁡(p(x)q(x))dx≃5.00×101
